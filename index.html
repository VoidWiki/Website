The Fossa's Story

This is the story of the fossa.

Once upon a time, a fossa named Rick was born. All he wanted in life was to be with his family and live happily. Sadly, his father left him before he was born, leaving him with an incomplete family consisting of his mother and his 4 siblings.

Rick soon became motivated to become a doctor, but he was only a few weeks old, so he depended on his mother to help him survive. After a few more weeks, his fur began to darken, and his eyes finally opened. He saw his brothers and sisters, all older than him, being taken care of by his mother.

Rick felt mad. This was his mother. He wouldn't let them be cared for by his mother. He opened his mouth, and to his surprise, a horrible high-pitched mewling noise came out. His mother turned around to look at him. Rick had finally found a way to get his mother's attention away from his brothers and sisters!

He continued mewling. Slowly, his mother began to ignore his siblings, and walk over to him. She fed him a piece of lemur arm affectionately, and then slapped him with her tail.

Rick's mother, like all fossa mothers, was a very cruel and unkind parent. She barely noticed her children as they grew older, and began mating with other fossas during the mating season. By the time he was a young adult, Rick had over 42 stepdads!

Rick and his siblings, tired of being ignored and clumped up with stepchildren in a tiny den, they decided to run away. On their 2nd birthday, they would escape from the den and continue their life dreams. Rick went to the northern part of the forest, and went to university to become a doctor. His 2 brothers decided to explore the forest and find their father. His 2 sisters went to the southern part of the forest, determined to make some friends and become fierce hunters.

Rick lived happily ever after, and even had a family of his own. He vowed to always treat his children fairly, and to never run away, no matter how tempting it ever felt.

This story is based on facts. Most of the information is from the San Diego Zoo. According to the zoo, "Fossa pups are very dependent on their mother for the first few weeks. Their eyes begin to open and their fur begins to darken when about two to three weeks old. The pups develop slowly and don't leave the den for about four to five months; they are dependent on their mother for another eight months. To get the mother's attention, the pups make a high-pitched noise called mewling. Fossa youngsters continue to grow until about two years of age. They then find a space of their own and usually only meet up with other fossas during the fall breeding season."

More Information

The fossa (Cryptoprocta ferox) is a long-tailed, cat-like mammal only living in Madagascar. It is a member of the family Eupleridae, and is closely related to the Malagasy Civet. It is the largest carnivorous mammal of Madagascar. "Over 50% of its diet is composed of lemurs".

The fossa's natural habitat is Madagascar. It has the most widespread geographical range of all Madagascar carnivorous mammals. The fossa is found in all forest habitats throughout Madagascar, it is seen more frequently in humid than in dry forests. The fossa is considered cathemeral. This means that a fossa "may experience activity peaks early in the morning, late in the afternoon, or late at night".

The diet of the fossa varies from small to medium-sized animals. Being a carnivorous mammal, the fossa is able to eat almost any animal smaller than it. Over half of the diets of fossas are lemur, and 5% of the diets are invertebrates. Fossas have "a polyandrous mating system". This type of mating is "very unique among carnivores".
